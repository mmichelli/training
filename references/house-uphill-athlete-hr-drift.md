## What we use this for

Practical methodology for the **aerobic threshold (AeT) estimation** that informs zone-setting. Provides the specific numerical thresholds (3.5%–5%) we cite in `principles.md` §4. NB: this is *practitioner literature*, not peer-reviewed — Steve House is a respected coach/alpinist, treat as field-tested heuristic.

## What the heart rate drift test measures

The "decoupling" between heart rate and pace during a sustained steady effort. As HR rises while pace stays the same, the percentage drift between the first and second half of the test indicates how close the chosen effort is to your aerobic threshold.

## Prerequisites

- Ability to maintain steady effort for 60 minutes.
- Conversational pace (talk in full sentences).
- Chest-strap or arm-band HR monitor.
- GPS watch or HR-connected smartphone.
- Treadmill (3% incline for jogging, 10%+ for hiking) or flat outdoor track.

## Protocol

1. **Warm-up (10–15 min):** Increase pace to just below previously known AeT (or until HR stabilizes).
2. **Test (40–60 min):** Hold a single steady, easy pace. Don't speed up, don't slow down.
3. **Record:** HR continuously. Note distance at halfway and at end (treadmill use).
4. **Cool down:** ~10 min easy.

## Calculation

```
drift% = ((avg HR second half / avg HR first half) − 1) × 100
```

Example: first half avg 144 bpm, second half avg 151 bpm → ((151/144) − 1) × 100 ≈ 4.9%.

## Interpretation

| Drift | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **3.5–5%** | Optimal — starting HR ≈ your AeT | Set this HR as top of Z2; top of Z1 = ~10% lower |
| **0–3.5%** | Within aerobic zones; pace too conservative | Retest at +5 bpm starting HR |
| **>5%** | Pace exceeded AeT | Retest at lower starting HR |

## Practical recommendations

- Frequency: every 4–6 months or when fitness shifts notably.
- Minimum duration 40 min; longer is more reliable.
- Wet HR strap contacts (water/saliva) for stable readings.
- Treadmill = ideal for controlled conditions; track = good for runners.

## How this constrains our claims

- ✓ Defensible: "HR drift between 3.5–5% identifies aerobic threshold." (Steve House's protocol — practitioner literature, repeatable methodology.)
- The "<5% = aerobic" framing in `principles.md` echoes Joe Friel's original threshold. House's range (3.5–5% optimal, with retests outside) is more nuanced; we should reflect that nuance when setting Mario's zones.
- This protocol is workable for Mario as soon as he can hold a steady 60-min effort — i.e. from about week 6–8 onwards.
