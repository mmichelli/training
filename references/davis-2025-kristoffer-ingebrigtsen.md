## Background

Kristoffer Ingebrigtsen — eldest of Norway's elite running family — went from sedentary, overweight 33-year-old to a 1:15 half-marathoner inside 18 months. Coached by his brother Henrik. Key differentiators from his Olympic-medalist brothers:

- **Single daily session** (not twice-daily).
- **Sub-threshold intervals**, not flat-out track repeats.
- **No specialized strength** (no hill repeats, drills).

This is what John Davis calls the "Norwegian model adapted for everyday runners."

## Henrik's philosophy quote

> "Start at a lower level, and work forward slowly instead of getting overconfident and going too hard."

## Typical weekly structure (as of 2021)

| Day | Workout | Distance |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Easy run | 12 km |
| Tue | Treadmill fartlek: 2 km w/u + **8 × 1000 m sub-threshold** with 500 m jog | ~12 km |
| Wed | Easy run | 12 km |
| Thu | **Threshold repeats: 2 km w/u + 4–5 × 2000 m sub-threshold** | ~14 km |
| Fri | Easy run | 12 km |
| Sat | **Stadium workout: 2 km w/u + 3 × 3000 m or 3 × 10 min efforts** | ~13 km |
| Sun | Long run | 16–18 km |
| **Total** | | **~95 km/week** |

The Saturday "stadium workout" is the one that breaks the "never above Z3" framing. 3 × 3000m or 3 × 10 min is *probably* threshold (Z4), not sub-threshold (top Z3).

## Specific paces (Kristoffer's, circa 2021)

- **Sub-threshold reps:** ~3:50/km — a 1:15 half-marathoner's sub-threshold.
- **Easy runs:** ~6:30/km initially; pace deliberately controlled with GPS / treadmill to prevent unconscious acceleration.

## Progression timeline

| Month | Phase |
|---|---|
| Feb 2020 | First calf strain — early attempts interrupted |
| Mar–May 2020 | Cross-training (20 min elliptical) alternating with 20-min easy runs |
| Jun–Dec 2020 | Easy-run base; volume to 30–40 min |
| Jan–Feb 2021 | Treadmill fartleks introduced |
| 1 Apr 2021 | First half-marathon: **1:29:52** |
| May 2021 | 10 K race: **37:15** (slightly ill) |
| Aug 2021 | Stavanger half: **1:20:53** (PB, 9-min improvement in 3 months) |
| Oct 2021 | Valencia half: **1:15:50** (PB) |

## Dietary notes

Kristoffer made simultaneous diet + training changes — eliminated breakfast-skipping, cut processed food and beer, shifted to lean protein + vegetables + skyr.

> "It's tough doing everything at once, but it's best to change diet and training simultaneously."

## Advice for recreational runners

1. Build to 8–10 km easy running *before* introducing intervals.
2. Use treadmill / GPS to prevent unconscious pace creep.
3. **Volume over intensity** — additional reps at slightly easier paces rather than maximal efforts.
4. Rhythm before intensity — consistent training pattern first, structured workouts second.
5. Avoid early speed work — risks injury and mental burnout.

> "Better to run a few more repetitions and rather run a bit slower… than running too hard."

## Davis's commentary

Kristoffer's approach is **applying Norwegian *principles* rather than copying elite schedules**. The single daily session, sub-threshold-not-redline intervals, and absence of strength specialty are the adaptation for a working person with finite time and recovery.

## How this constrains our claims

- ✓ Defensible: "Quality on Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday" — the Tue/Thu/Sat shape.
- ✓ Defensible: "Lower-intensity than traditional periodized plans."
- ✓ Defensible: "Volume over intensity, consistency over peaking."
- ⚠ **Overclaim:** "Never above top of Z3." The Saturday stadium workout (3×3000m or 3×10 min) is not described as sub-threshold. Our principles doc should call this honestly: *our* plan goes further than Kristoffer's by capping at sub-threshold even on Saturday — justified by Mario's knee history and being 14 years older.
- The pace anchor (~3:50/km sub-threshold) is irrelevant for Mario in the short term — his sub-threshold will be much slower; calibrate via 5K parkrun benchmark + HR.
