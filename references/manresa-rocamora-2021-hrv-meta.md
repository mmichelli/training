# Heart Rate Variability-Guided Training for Enhancing Cardiac-Vagal Modulation, Aerobic Fitness, and Endurance Performance: A Methodological Systematic Review with Meta-Analysis

**Authors:** Agustín Manresa-Rocamora, José Manuel Sarabia, Alejandro Javaloyes, Andrew A. Flatt, Manuel Moya-Ramón
**Journal:** International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health
**Year:** 2021
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph181910299
**Source URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8507742/
**Fetched:** 2026-05-14

---

## What we use this for

Foundation for the HRV-guided readiness model in `principles.md` §4 and the traffic-light rule in §5. The honest finding is **"fewer hard sessions, similar performance"** — *not* "HRV-guided always wins," which would be overclaiming.

## Abstract

HRV-guided training was superior for enhancing vagal-related HRV indices (SMD+ = 0.50, 95% CI 0.09–0.91), but not resting HR, when compared to predefined training. Small but non-significant improvements favored HRV-guided training for maximal aerobic capacity, aerobic capacity at ventilatory thresholds, and endurance performance. The review concludes that HRV-guided training may be more effective than predefined approaches for maintaining vagal-mediated HRV with fewer negative responses, though fitness and performance advantages appear marginal if they exist.

## Methods

- PRISMA-compliant systematic review with random-effects meta-analysis.
- Databases: Web of Science, PubMed, Embase through October 2020.
- 8 studies / 10 analysis units / 199 participants comparing HRV-guided vs predefined endurance training prescriptions.
- Moderator variables: HRV index (RMSSD, SD1, HF), recording position, assessment duration, baseline reference criteria.

## Key results

### Cardiac-vagal modulation

- RMSSD/SD1: SMD+ = 0.50 (95% CI 0.09–0.91) — favors HRV-guided.
- HF: SMD+ = −0.60 (95% CI −1.15 to −0.05) — opposite direction.
- Resting HR: SMD+ = 0.04 (95% CI −0.34 to 0.43) — no difference.

### Aerobic fitness

- VO₂max: SMD+ = 0.13 (95% CI −0.12 to 0.39) — non-significant.
- Maximal aerobic capacity: SMD+ = 0.20 (95% CI −0.07 to 0.47) — non-significant.
- Aerobic capacity at VT2: SMD+ = 0.26 (95% CI −0.05 to 0.57) — non-significant.

### Endurance performance

- Overall SMD+ = 0.20 (95% CI −0.09 to 0.48) — **non-significant**.
- Heterogeneity: I² = 0.0% across fitness and performance outcomes.

## Conclusion

HRV-guided training is *superior* for maintaining vagal-mediated HRV with fewer negative individual responses. For fitness/performance, advantages are "only by a small margin." Methodological inconsistencies across studies (assessment position, index selection, baseline reference criteria) limit stronger inferences and motivate standardization in future work.

## How this constrains our claims

- ✓ Defensible: "HRV-guided lets you do fewer hard sessions and reach similar fitness."
- ✗ Overclaim: "HRV-guided beats fixed plans on performance." (The meta-analysis says non-significant.)
- The single trial that *did* show 3000m improvement (2.1 vs 1.1%) is Vesterinen 2016, not this meta-analysis.
