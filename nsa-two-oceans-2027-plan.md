# Couch to Two Oceans 2027 — Norwegian Singles Approach

## What changed

Out: Parry's phase structure with dedicated hill weeks and intensity blocks.
In: NSA — sub-threshold quality 3× per week, every week, year-round.

NSA is what Kristoffer Ingebrigtsen used to go from overweight 33-year-old to 1:15 half marathon in 18 months. His profile mirrors yours: full-time job, two young kids, renovating, history of getting hurt when he ran hard. The structure protects you from yourself.

**Core rule of NSA: you never go hard.** Sub-threshold = top of Z3, never Z4. Every rep ends feeling like you could do another. Heart rate ceiling, treadmill pace ceiling, watch discipline. Going harder doesn't get you fitter; it gets you injured.

Hill insurance kept in. One short hill session every 3rd week through the build phases, and longer hill work in the final ultra-specific block, because Constantia Nek demands quad-specific strength that flat sub-threshold work alone won't give you.

## The NSA week (canonical, once you're up to full volume)

| Day | Session | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Easy 60 min Z2 | Aerobic, recovery |
| **Tue** | Sub-threshold intervals (short reps) | Threshold development |
| **Wed** | Easy 60 min Z2 + Gym A | Aerobic + strength |
| **Thu** | Sub-threshold intervals (medium reps) | Threshold development |
| **Fri** | Easy 60 min Z2 + Gym B | Aerobic + strength |
| **Sat** | Sub-threshold intervals (long reps) OR hill session | Threshold or quad-strength |
| **Sun** | Long run Z2 (90 min → 4 hours) | Endurance |

That's the shape. Volume builds inside this skeleton. Quality stays controlled.

**Wednesday CrossFit at work:** Replace the Wednesday easy run with CrossFit, but tell the coach to scale lower-body intensity (no max squats, no heavy box jumps, no AMRAP burpees). You want it to be Z3 cardio plus some upper-body strength, not legs-destroyed. If a Wednesday CrossFit class would compromise Thursday's sub-threshold, skip it that week.

**Spin classes / home spin bike:** Use to swap an easy run when knee is grumpy or weather is brutal. Z2-Z3 effort, not redline. Best as Wednesday or Friday substitute, never Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday.

## Heart-rate zones — set these in week 2

Karvonen: HRR = HRmax − HRrest. Zone = HRrest + (HRR × %).

- Z1: 50–60% HRR (very easy, recovery)
- **Z2: 60–70% HRR (easy aerobic — most running)**
- Z3: 70–80% HRR (steady; **top of Z3 = sub-threshold ceiling**)
- Z4: 80–90% HRR (threshold — STAY OUT in NSA)
- Z5: 90–100% HRR (VO2 — never go here)

**Sub-threshold target: 75-78% HRR.** Use this as your ceiling on all quality reps. Lactate would be ~2.0-3.0 mmol/L (you don't need a meter; HR + perceived effort suffice).

**Talk test for sub-threshold:** Short phrases, not full sentences. Not gasping. Could keep going for another 5 reps if you had to. If you can't hold a 3-word phrase, you've crossed into Z4 and you're doing it wrong.

HRmax test in week 3: 10 min easy w/u → 3 × 3 min uphill all-out, 2 min jog recovery between → highest reading in the third rep is close to true max. **Not** 220-age.

- Thu: **Sub-threshold session** + Gym B

## Race day strategy (unchanged from before)

Target: 6:30-6:50 finish (Blue medal with margin). Average 7:00-7:15/km.

- **0-28 km:** 9:1 run-walk, ~7:00/km, controlled. Don't bank time, protect quads.
- **28-35 km Chapman's Peak:** Walk Little Chappies. Run-walk main Chappies at conservative effort. Run the descent into Hout Bay relaxed.
- **35-47 km to Constantia summit:** Back to 9:1. On the final 2 km steep section, walk with pride.
- **47-56 km finish:** Resume 9:1, enjoy the descent, UCT awaits.

**Fuel:** Pre-race oats + banana + coffee. Gel every 5 km from km 8. Coke from aid stations after km 35. Bananas anytime. Salt tab every 90 min if warm.

## Gym program (Mon/Wed alternating, or shift to Tue/Fri once NSA starts)

**Session A — Lower body strength:**
1. Back squat — 3 × 5, add 2.5 kg per session (Starting Strength linear progression)
2. Romanian deadlift — 3 × 8
3. Bulgarian split squat — 3 × 8 each leg
4. Plank — 3 × 30-60s
5. Pull-up or lat pulldown — 3 × 8

**Session B — Posterior chain + accessory:**
1. Hip thrust — 3 × 8
2. Single-leg calf raise — 3 × 12 each
3. Glute bridge with band — 3 × 12
4. Side plank — 3 × 30-45s each
5. Single-arm row — 3 × 8 each
6. Eccentric step-down — 3 × 10 each leg

**Form basics — book one trainer session in week 1.** Non-negotiable. Squat depth, RDL hinge, hip thrust setup.

**Strength taper:** Drop to maintenance volume (1 set per exercise) from week 45 onwards. Skip the gym entirely in race week.

## Weekly check-in rule

Every Sunday evening:
1. Slept 7+ hours per night?
2. Knee equal to or better than last Sunday?
3. Saturday long run felt like I could have done more?
4. Hit both gym sessions?
5. Did sub-threshold sessions feel controlled (not crushing)?

3+ "yes" = continue as planned. 2 or fewer = **next week is automatically a down-week**. No ego, no negotiation.

## What makes this plan different from the Parry version

| Element | Parry version | NSA version |
|---|---|---|
| Quality intensity | Z3-Z4 (threshold/tempo) | Z3 ceiling only (sub-threshold) |
| Hill work | Dedicated 8-week phase | Insurance dose every 3rd week + ultra-specific block |
| Weekly structure | Varies by phase | Same shape week to week |
| Recovery within sessions | Modest jog | Full walk between reps (impact reduction) |
| Mental load | Hard sessions to dread | "Could do another rep" — no dread |
| Knee risk | Higher (threshold + hills) | Lower (controlled pace ceiling) |
| Time efficiency | Variable | Consistent ~6-10 hr/wk |

The NSA bet: you can build genuine threshold-area fitness without ever crossing into the territory that breaks deconditioned bodies. Kristoffer proved it. Sirpoc proved it. Your "weak" knee will be happy about it.

The Parry tradition isn't wrong — it's just optimised for athletes who don't have your specific injury history and life constraints. NSA was built for exactly your situation.

## Tracking

Garmin sync + dashboard live in this repo. Everything is plain markdown — no DB — so the data is readable by both you and any LLM you point at it.

**Daily/weekly workflow:**
1. After a run, watch syncs to Garmin Connect.
2. `uv run python sync.py` — pulls new activities into `activities/YYYY-MM-DD-<type>-<id>.md`. Re-runs are idempotent and preserve any hand-written notes under the `## Notes` heading of each file.
3. `uv run python dashboard.py` — regenerates `dashboard.md` with this-week vs. plan, an 8-week rolling volume table, recent quality sessions (with a flag if HR drifted above ~88% of observed max — i.e., you crossed into Z4), and the Sunday check-in scaffold.

**Plan anchor:** `PLAN_START` in `dashboard.py` is the Monday of plan week 1. Update it once you commit to a start date; the dashboard derives the current plan week from there.

**Per-activity notes:** open the activity file and fill the HTML comments — `<!-- felt: -->`, `<!-- knee: -->`, `<!-- sub-threshold controlled? -->`. These survive re-syncs. Future you (and future LLM context) will thank you.

**Calendar (Google Calendar subscription):**
- `make publish` (or `uv run python publish_calendar.py`) — regenerates `plan.ics` and pushes to the public repo.
- Subscribe URL: `https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mmichelli/training/main/plan.ics`
- In Google Calendar: Settings → Add calendar → From URL → paste. Re-publishing auto-updates events (Google refreshes ~daily).

**Garmin Fenix structured workouts** (`workouts.py`):
- Once Garmin auth is working, `make workouts` pushes the quality sessions (sub-threshold, hills, Constantia sims, tempos) as structured workouts onto the watch calendar. Easy days stay open.
- The Fenix doesn't surface generic Google Calendar events, hence this separate path.

**AI coach** (`coach.py`):
- `make coach` — Claude reads the plan, dashboard, and recent activity notes, writes a weekly check-in to `coach.md`. Verdict on volume, quality, recovery, and the call for the next week.
- `make ask Q="should I skip Tuesday?"` — ad-hoc coaching question.
- Uses the `claude` CLI under the hood, so no extra API key is needed.

**One-shot daily routine:** `make daily` → sync + dashboard + coach.

## Trust the structure

If you find yourself, six months from now, feeling unsporty during the Tuesday session because it's "not hard enough" — that's the plan working. The hard sessions show up as more reps and longer reps, not as faster reps. Your race day pace is determined by the work you do on Sundays and Thursdays, not by how hard you breathed last Tuesday.

The plan is the plan. Trust it.
